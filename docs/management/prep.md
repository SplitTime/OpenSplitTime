# Preparation

## Going Public and Going Live

Each Event Group is either "public" or "private." Public event groups can be seen by everyone, but private event
groups are viewable only by the Organization Owner and its designated Stewards.

Each Event Group is either "available live" or not "available live." When an Event Group is available live, it is
ready to accept Raw Times entered from the Live Entry screen (**Live > Time Entry**) or
from [OST Remote](https://www.opensplittime.org).

## Going Public (or Private)

**When you create a new Event Group, it is private**, allowing you time to load your splits and
entrants and get everything ready. An Event Group will not become public until you make it so.

You can change an Event Group from private to public by using the Construction Status page (**Admin > Construction > Status > Go
Public**).

You can also change an Event Group back to private from the Construction Status page (**Admin > Construction > Status > Take
Private**).

## Enabling and Disabling Live Entry

**When you create a new Event Group is it not available live**. This allows you time to get
everything set up before it becomes visible to users of OST Remote. An Event Group will not be enabled for live
entry until you make it so.

You can enable live entry for an Event Group from the Construction Status Page (**Admin > Construction > Status > Enable
Live Entry**).

You can also disable live entry for an Event Group from the Construction Status Page (**Admin > Construction > Status > Disable
Live Entry**).

## Helping People Follow Your Event

OpenSplitTime gives you access to free, convenient updates for families, friends, fans, and crews. Updates are
available at the Results pages and also via text message and email updates to subscribed users.

To make sure everyone knows how to follow your Event Group live, we provide an easy guide customized for each
Event Group. To inform your Entrants how to follow the event, visit the Follow page (**Results >
Follow**), copy the link, and send it to your contact list.

**Note that the Follow page, like the rest of your event pages, will not be visible to the general public
until you have made your Event Group public.** Make sure to
[make your event public](https://www.opensplittime.org) before sending any links
intended for public use.

## Obtaining Timing Equipment

If you plan to record times using [OST Remote](https://www.opensplittime.org), you will need to gather enough
iOS devices (iPhones and iPads) for use at the various stations that will be recording times. When determining
the number of devices you need, **remember to account for any stations that will need two devices**,
for example, if you will be recording times both in and out of the station and the course is arranged such that
participants check into and out of the station at different physical locations.

You can buy devices for your Organization, share with another Organization whose event schedule is compatible
with yours, or borrow devices from friends of the Organization. Make sure you obtain your devices with plenty of
time before your event to ensure you can get OST Remote loaded and train your timing crews.

## Device Requirements

OST Remote requires an iPhone or iPad running iOS 9 or later.

We have found that an iPad Mini is about the right size for easy viewing and easy handling. A medium-format
(9.5- to 11-inch) iPad is also fine, though not quite as easy to use while standing up. Large (13-inch) iPads will
work fine for an operator at a table, but they are awkward to use while standing.

Newer devices will result in slightly better feel and performance, but any iOS device produced since about 2012
will run OST Remote. As a rule of thumb, **any device that uses a Lightning port** (the small,
reversible Apple charging port) will probably work; any device with an older 30-pin connector will probably not
work.

Most iPhones will also work, though those with smaller screens will be harder to read.

## Buying Your Own

As of 2023, new iPad Minis are available from Apple starting from $499 in the US. Most Organizations will
prefer to buy used devices from a reputable used equipment supplier. As of 2023, **a used iPad Mini in fair
condition should cost around $50 for a Gen 2, $80 for a Gen 3, or $120 for a Gen 4** in the US.

We have had good luck buying devices from
[Swappa](https://www.opensplittime.org),
[Gazelle](https://www.opensplittime.org), and
[eBay](https://www.opensplittime.org).

## Protecting Your Devices

You should prepare your devices for weather and rough treatment. It is likely that during a given event, some
device will be dropped. We strongly recommend that you **place each device into a protective case**
designed for outdoor use.

Also consider weatherproofing as appropriate for wet conditions. In the rain, a large ziplock bag can be
useful.

Remember that batteries are less effective (sometimes dramatically less effective) in cold conditions.
**In very cold temperatures, a weak battery may suddenly fail**, resulting in a device that reports
50% or more battery shutting off without warning.

In temperatures below 50 degrees, try to keep devices warm by keeping them inside a pocket or otherwise close to
body heat, such as inside a jacket. During extended use in cold conditions, keep an external battery inside your
jacket with the cable attached to the device at all times.

## Battery Life

You will need to make sure each device has an acceptable battery life for your event. Older devices tend to have
older, weaker batteries, but this depends on other factors such as the amount of use. Regardless of battery
capacity, **we strongly recommend that you have an external power source and charging cable
available** at all stations when using OST Remote.

All devices use power when they are turned on, even when in sleep mode. When storing your devices for more than a
day or two, power the device off. For an iPad or iPad Mini, use the **on/off/sleep button**, which is
located at the top right corner of the device.

1. Press and hold the **on/off/sleep button** for a few seconds until a slider appears on the
screen.

1. Move the "Slide to power off" slider all the way to the right.

1. You may see a spinning wheel for a few seconds. The screen will go dark after a few more seconds.

1. Test that the iPad is powered off by pressing the Home button a few times. The device should not respond.

To turn an iPad back on, hold the **on/off/sleep button** for a few seconds until the Apple logo
appears.

## Software Updates

Make sure to check for system software updates regularly. On your iOS device, while you are connected to a WiFi
network, go to **Settings > General > Software Update** and the device will tell you if a system
software update is available.

Also make sure you are always using the latest version of OST Remote. On your iOS device, tap
**App Store > Updates** and the device will tell you if a newer version of OST Remote is available.

## Training Timing Crews

Event timing can be intense. A trained timing crew will be less stressed, more accurate, and generally more
effective than an untrained crew. Please make an effort to train your crews in advance.

## Creating Steward Accounts

First, you will need one or more timing accounts authorized as Stewards for your organization. Follow these
steps:

1. **Log out from your own account** (you cannot create a new account while logged in)

1. Click the Sign Up button

1. Enter an email address and password. Remember that you will need access to the email address you use so that
you can confirm the account. Make sure you write down or otherwise store the email and password in a secure
place.

1. Log back in to your own account

1. Authorize the new time entry accounts as Stewards (**My Stuff > Your Organization > Stewards**).
You must have Owner privileges for your Organization in order to grant Steward privileges to another account.

Creating Steward accounts is a one-time process. You can re-use them in future years. Consider changing your
passwords between events to discourage unauthorized use.

## Creating a Test Event Group

For training purposes, your crews will need to have access to an Event Group. Follow these steps:

1. Create an Event Group using the usual [Construction process](https://www.opensplittime.org).
**Note: Do not make your mock Event Group public!** (It will be confusing to the public if you do.)

1. Make your mock Event Group available live (**Admin > Construction > Group Actions > Enable Live**). Remember that
**OST Remote will not recognize an Event Group unless it is enabled for live entry**.

1. Verify that your mock event is available for time entry by logging in to OST Remote using a Steward account.
The mock Event Group should appear in the "Select Event" list.

We recommend that each Organization create one or more mock Event Groups to simulate actual race conditions. Mock
Event Groups should always be kept private, and never made available for public view. Once you have created a mock
Event Group, you can enable or disable live entry as often as you like. **Consider keeping a mock Event
Group available for training in later years** so you do not have to re-create one for each new event.

## Using the Test Event Group

Timing crews will do best if they actually enter and sync some times in your mock Event Group. Follow these
steps:

1. Provide an email and password for a Steward account to your timing crews. Or, if you prefer to keep your
Steward passwords secret, simply log in to OST Remote and provide devices to your crews. The password will not
have to be re-entered unless an operator logs out and attempts to change the password when logging back in.

1. Direct your timing crews to the [OST Remote Documentation](https://www.opensplittime.org), and ask them to
read it carefully and test OST Remote. Encourage them to enter and sync times. Nothing will be visible to the
public so long as the Event Group remains private.

1. If you are able, hold an in-person meeting or video conference with timing crews to make sure they understand
how to use OST Remote

## Cleaning Up

After training is complete, you will need to disable the mock Event Group and clean up. Follow these steps:

1. Make sure all devices are logged out of OST Remote

1. Disable live entry for your mock Event Group (**Admin > Construction > Group Actions > Disable Live**). This step is
critical to avoid any accidental use of your mock Event Group during the actual event.

1. Verify that the mock Event Group no longer appears when attempting to log in to OST Remote.

## Predicting Traffic

If you have data from at least one prior Event on the same Course, you can predict the timing and density of
traffic through your stations. Predictive data can help you determine when your aid stations need to open, when
they can expect to close down, and how many Entrants to expect at various times during the open period.

From a prior Event Group, visit the Traffic screen (**Results > Traffic**). You will see a list of
time intervals and counts, indicating how many Entrants were recorded at the starting station during the various
time intervals.

Select another station by using the **station dropdown menu** or clicking the arrows on either side
of the station dropdown menu.

For stations that record both "in" and "out" times, there will be separate "in" and "out" counts for each time
interval.

Select a different time interval using the **interval dropdown menu**.

## Setting a Notice on the Results Page

Sometimes it is helpful to alert followers to special conditions about your race. For example, you may want to notify people
that times at a particular aid station will not be available live, or that crew access to an aid station requires 4WD because
of unexpected road conditions.

You can set such a message to appear on the Results page by going to **Construction > Events and Courses**, then click
on the applicable Event icon, and enter the message in the **Notice Text** field. Once set, the notice text will appear
prominently on the Full Results page for the Event.
