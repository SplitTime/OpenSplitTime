# Menu

## Revealing and Hiding the Main Menu

Let's start by getting familiar with the Main Menu. You can reveal the Main Menu in either of two ways:

1. In the upper right corner you should see the word "Menu" with a stack icon next to it. Tap the
word or the icon.

1. From anywhere in the app, touch the screen and swipe to the left.

You can hide the Main Menu by tapping "Close" or by swiping back to the right.

Practice revealing and hiding the Main Menu using both methods to see which works better for you.
**You want this action to feel like second nature**, as you will need to use it quite often
during
your time entry shift.

## Main Menu Items

The Main Menu has five items:

1. **Live Entry**: For live time entry. You will spend most of your time on this screen.

1. **Review/Sync**: Where you review entries and sync them with the server.

1. **Cross Check**: An overview of all the bib numbers and their status at your aid station.

1. **Change Station**: Use this if your device is moving from one aid station to another.

1. **Logout**: Use this only if you need to change to a different event or log in as a
different
user.

We'll go over each menu item in detail in the next few pages.

## Live Entry

The Live Entry screen is your primary workhorse. It is designed for one purpose, which is to quickly and
accurately enter bib numbers as runners pass through a time entry point. To enter a time, simply tap in a
bib number using the keypad, then tap a large blue submit button to submit the time.

When you enter a bib number, the entrant's name will display near the top of the screen. Feel free to give
them a shout out! This is a great way to ensure you've entered the right bib number.

**OST Remote uses sound effects to improve the accuracy of your time entry.** Enable the sound
on your device using a physical switch located on the left or right side of the iPhone or iPad. Adjust the
volume to an appropriate level. You should hear sound effects when you tap the keypad and the submit button. The
audio feedback will greatly assist you in knowing when your taps are being recognized by the device. This can be
especially helpful when the weather is bad or when you are working in direct sunlight.

## Submit Button(s)

If your aid station is set up for a single time entry point, you will see one blue submit button extending
across the screen.

If your aid station is set up for two time entry points, you will see two blue submit buttons, one for each
time entry point. This would be the case, for example, if you are recording runners both "in" and "out" of
your aid station, or if runners are passing through your aid station at different times during the event.

If you have two buttons on your live entry screen,
**make sure you are familiar with which button does what**, and always use the correct one!
This will save a great deal of time and energy when times are synced.

## Special Keys

If you make a mistake **while entering a bib number but before tapping the blue button**, you can
correct it using the delete key (&#9003;) on the keypad.

If you are not able to read a bib number, tap the asterisk key (*) on the keypad. If you read only a part
of the bib number, use the asterisk to substitute for the missing portion. For example, if you can read a "2"
but aren't able to determine the rest of the bib number, type "2*" and tap the submit button.

## Quick Edit

After you submit an entry, the runner name stays visible until you use the keypad again. If you catch a mistake
**after tapping the blue button but before you touch the keypad again**, you can quickly edit or
delete the entry by tapping the runner name, making necessary changes, and tapping "Update" or "Delete."

## Continuing or Done?

If a participant comes into the aid station and immediately tells you he or she intends not to continue,
enter the bib number, then tap "Stopped Here," then tap the blue submit button.

If you have already recorded a time for a participant, and you later learn that he or she will not
continue, **do not re-enter the bib number from the Live Entry screen.** Instead, use the Review/Sync
screen to edit the entry you already have for that bib number.

## Monitoring Pacers

If your event is monitoring pacers, you will have a toggle button to indicate if the bib number you are
recording is accompanied by a pacer. Simply enter the bib number, then tap "With Pacer" before tapping the
blue submit button.

If you have already recorded a time for a participant, and you later learn that he or she has a pacer,
**do not re-enter the bib number from the Live Entry screen.** Instead, use the Review/Sync
screen to edit the entry you already have for that bib number.

## Review / Sync

**Times entered in Live Entry exist only locally within the device until you sync the data
using the Review/Sync screen.** Prior to being synced, time entries cannot be seen by the timing crew
at race headquarters or by anyone following the event online.

This is both a blessing and a curse. On the upside, it allows you to review your data and make sure it is
accurate before sending it to the OpenSplitTime server for processing. On the downside, it means you will need to
think about reviewing and synchronizing your data early and often.

At the bottom of the Review/Sync screen is a green Sync button. If any entries are not synced, you will see a
red badge indicating how many unsynced times there are. Verify that the unsynced entries are correct, then tap
the Sync button to sync time entries with the server.

## Reviewing Your Data

The Review/Sync screen presents a list of all times you have entered using that device. The color of the entry
and symbols shown with it indicate the status:

1. Entries that **have been synced** are shown in **green**

1. Entries with **known bib numbers that have not been synced** are shown in **black**

1. Entries with **unknown bib numbers that have not been synced** are shown in **red**

1. Entries indicating the entrant is **done** will show a **hand icon**

1. Entries indicating the entrant is **with a pacer** will show a
**double-runner icon** (this option is available only
for races that elect to monitor pacers)

You can sort your data by Time Entered, Time Displayed, Name, or Bib Number. To change the sort, touch the "Sort
By" selector at the top of the screen and select a sort method using the scroll wheel at the bottom of the
screen.

## Syncing Your Data

If you have no internet connection available, you will need to sync data whenever you return to a location with a
connection. If you have a spare device, you might be able to shuttle to an area with connectivity and use the
spare device while the primary device is in transit. In the worst case, you will be able to sync data after
the event is concluded.

If you have an internet connection at your aid station, you should sync as often as reasonably possible. Remember
that friends, family, and crews following online cannot see the runner times until you sync.
**Try not to let times sit for more than a few minutes without syncing**.

If the internet connection is poor, a sync may take several seconds to complete. If runners are approaching while
a sync is going on, you can always tap “Return to Live Entry” and the sync will finish in the background.
If the internet connection is intermittent, a sync may fail. Try again. Times will not be marked as “synced” in
your device until OST Remote has received confirmation from the server that the sync was successful.

## Editing Entries

You can edit an entry from the Review/Sync screen by tapping on the entry. If the entry has not been synced, you
can edit the entry directly. If the entry has already been synced, you can create a duplicate entry and edit the
duplicate.

**If you make a mistake, you should edit the faulty entry through the Review/Sync screen instead of creating
a new entry through the Live Entry screen**. To edit, go to Review/Sync and tap the entry in question.

If the entry has not been synced, you can update or delete the entry, or tap "Cancel" to return to Review/Sync
without making changes.

If the entry has already been synced, OST Remote will ask you to verify that you want to create a duplicate. Tap
"Yes," OST Remote will create a new entry, and you will be taken to the Edit screen. Once there, you can change
duplicate entry as needed and tap "Create New Entry" when finished. You can tap "Cancel" to return to Review/Sync
without saving the duplicate entry. To maintain data integrity,
**OST Remote does not allow an entry to be deleted after it has been synced**.

## Cross Check

The Cross Check screen gives you an instant report summarizing the status of each bib number at your aid station.
You will use Cross Check to verify that all runners have been recorded or otherwise accounted for at your
station.

Bib numbers are color coded to show status at a glance:

1. Bibs recorded at your station are marked "Recorded" and shown in green

1. Bibs that you recorded as not continuing from your station are marked "Stopped" and shown in red

1. Bibs that did not start the event, or that stopped at a previous station, or that were missed at your station
and recorded later, are marked “Not Expected” and shown in black

1. All other bib numbers are marked "Expected" and shown in blue

## Automatic Flagging

Each time you visit the Cross Check screen, OST Remote will attempt to contact the OpenSplitTime servers. If you
are operating with an internet connection, the servers will transmit information about participants all around the
course, and OST Remote will use that information to automatically flag bibs as Expected or Not Expected.

**If OST Remote is able to reach the servers, it will overwrite any manual flagging of Expected or Not
Expected bib numbers you may have made within OST Remote.** This is a good thing. The OpenSplitTime server
will have the big picture and should be relied on as the ultimate source of truth. If the server is giving you
incorrect information, you should contact headquarters and ask them to correct it via the website.

## Manual Flagging

If you are operating without an internet connection, you can manually set bib numbers as Expected or Not
Expected, based on information you receive from headquarters by other means, such as radio communications. For
example, you might call and ask for a list of bib numbers that did not start, and you can mark each of these as
"Not Expected" in your Cross Check screen.

To change status, tap "Bulk Select," then tap the bib numbers you want to change, then tap "Expected" or "Not
Expected" at the bottom of the screen. You can change an Expected bib number to Not Expected or vice versa. You
cannot change the status of a bib number that is Recorded or Stopped, as these categories are determined solely by
analyzing the data entered locally on your device.

## Filtering

Using the buttons at the bottom of the Cross Check screen, you can filter bib numbers to show only a single
category, such as only Expected or only Recorded. This makes it easy to determine which runners are not accounted
for, particularly near the end of your aid station's shift.

Use the grey "Show all" button in the lower right corner to return to showing all bib numbers.

## Change Station

If you move from one aid station to another during an event, or if you receive a device that someone else has
been using at another aid station, you will need to switch to the correct aid station before recording times.

Simply tap the Menu icon, tap "Change Station," select the correct aid station, tap "Done," and tap "Live Entry."

## Logout

The final Main Menu item is Logout. To use it, reveal the Main Menu, tap "Logout," and then tap "Logout" again in
the confirmation dialog box.

You will probably only ever need to use this feature if

1. You need to log in with a different username

1. You need to switch to a different event

1. The event has concluded and you want to make sure no additional times are inadvertently added

Use this item with care. Once you are logged out, you will not have any way to log back in unless you have an
active internet connection. Because of this, the Logout feature is disabled if OST Remote senses that
your device does not have an active internet connection.

**If your aid station does not have a reliable internet connection, make sure you are logged in before
heading into the wilderness!**
